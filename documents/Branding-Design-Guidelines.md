# THEESMA SPORTS — BRANDING & DESIGN GUIDELINES

## 1. Brand Overview

Theesma Sports is a performance-driven sports brand built by athletes, for athletes.
The platform combines e-commerce with real tournament credibility.

---

## 2. Brand Positioning

- Bold
- Sporty
- Performance-focused
- Athlete-first
- Experience-driven

Tagline:
Built for Performance. Proven in Competition.

---

## 3. Brand Personality

Primary Tone:
- Aggressive
- High-energy
- Competitive

Secondary Tone:
- Professional
- Trustworthy
- Inspirational

---

## 4. Visual Identity

### Colors

Primary:
- Black (#000000)
- White (#FFFFFF)

Accent:
- Electric Blue (#1E90FF)
- Energy Orange (#FF6A00)

Usage:
- Dark backgrounds for premium feel
- Accent colors for highlights & CTAs

---

## 5. Typography

Headings:
- Bold, modern sans-serif (e.g., Inter / Poppins)
- Uppercase preferred

Body:
- Clean sans-serif
- High readability

---

## 6. UI Design Principles

- Mobile-first design
- Clean layouts with strong hierarchy
- High contrast for readability
- Minimal clutter

---

## 7. Motion & Interaction

Tools:
- GSAP ScrollTrigger
- Spline (3D)

Guidelines:
- Motion should enhance storytelling
- Avoid excessive animations
- Keep transitions smooth and fast

---

## 8. 3D Usage

- Use for hero products
- Allow rotate + zoom
- Provide fallback for mobile

Rule:
3D must help buying decision, not distract

---

## 9. Imagery Style

- Real athletes preferred
- Action-focused visuals
- High contrast lighting
- Dynamic angles

---

## 10. UX Strategy

Homepage Flow:
- Hero (3D + bold statement)
- Storytelling sections
- Product highlights
- Events credibility
- Final CTA

---

## 11. Conversion Guidelines

- Strong CTA buttons
- Urgency indicators (stock, timers)
- Social proof (reviews)
- Easy checkout (one-page)

---

## 12. Do’s & Don’ts

Do:
- Keep it bold and sporty
- Focus on performance storytelling

Don’t:
- Use generic stock layouts
- Overload with animations
- Break mobile experience

---

## 13. Final Principle

Every design decision should answer:

“Does this help an athlete feel confident to buy?”

If yes → keep it  
If no → remove it
